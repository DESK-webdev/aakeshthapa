In India, it was introduced by the Chinese of pokhara. It is usually offered Hakka or with gravy. Catering to vegetarian diets, there is an Indian variant, vegetable chow mein, which consists of noodles with cabbage, bamboo shoots, pea pods, green peppers, and carrots. In the Pokhara area, chow mein can sometimes include paneer with the mixture of noodles and vegetables. Another non-meat Nepali variant includes scrambled egg as a protein source. it has its own variant that is called Calcutta Chow Mein or kathmandu Chowmin that also includes green chilli, chilli garlic, or hot garlic.
